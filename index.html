Vision

Architecture student.
Tetovo, North Macedonia.

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Architecture and Engineering at the University of Tetovo, where I have been studying since 2022 and expect to graduate in 2027. Throughout my academic journey, I have developed projects across a variety of architectural typologies, including sports facilities, educational buildings, commercial spaces, residential interiors, and public environments. Each project has strengthened my ability to balance functionality, context, and design while responding to the needs of its users.

I am particularly interested in the subtle elements of architecture that often go unnoticed, such as thresholds, courtyards, and the relationship between light, shadow, and material. To me, stone, concrete, and timber are more than construction materials; they are a language through which architecture expresses character, atmosphere, and identity. I believe that meaningful design emerges from careful observation, thoughtful detailing, and a deep understanding of how people experience space.

How I
work

Each project moves through seven phases. The path between them is never linear.

01

Listen

Site visits, client conversations, contextual study. The project begins before the first sketch.

02

Concept

Hand drawings and physical models. Spatial logic emerges through iteration.

03

Model

Digital precision meets spatial intuition. Every decision tested in three dimensions.

04

Visualise

Renders communicate the design before a single brick is laid.

05

Technical

Construction documents, specifications, engineering coordination.

06

On Site

Close involvement during construction. Design integrity protected to the last detail.

07

Legacy

The key in the door — and a building that will outlast this conversation.
