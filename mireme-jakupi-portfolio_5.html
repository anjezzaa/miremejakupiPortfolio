Architecture student.
Tetovo, North Macedonia.

Mireme Jakupi is an architecture student at South East European University in Tetovo, North Macedonia, where she has been studying since 2022 and will graduate in 2027. Her work spans sports facilities, cafes, educational buildings, residential interiors and public spaces.

She is drawn to the parts of a building that are easy to overlook. Thresholds, courtyards, the way light falls at a certain hour. Stone, concrete, and timber are not just materials to her. They are the vocabulary of a building's character.

Project Notes

A redesign of the main public square in Tetovo, with the goal of creating a modern, functional and inclusive space for everyone in the city. The concept brings together recreational areas, a children's playground, green spaces, public seating, a decorative fountain and pedestrian paths that connect the different parts of the square. The design encourages people to gather, relax and enjoy being outdoors, giving the city centre a fresh contemporary identity.

How I
work

Each project moves through seven phases. The path between them is never linear.

01

Listen

Site visits, client conversations, contextual study. The project begins before the first sketch.

02

Concept

Hand drawings and physical models. Spatial logic emerges through iteration.

03

Model

Digital precision meets spatial intuition. Every decision tested in three dimensions.

04

Visualise

Renders communicate the design before a single brick is laid.

05

Technical

Construction documents, specifications, engineering coordination.

06

On Site

Close involvement during construction. Design integrity protected to the last detail.

07

Legacy

The key in the door — and a building that will outlast this conversation.
